## Where to start?

AI in an SMB is not a big transformation project. It's a repetitive task that <span className="corail">disappears on a Monday morning</span>.

The 5 cases below come from French SMBs with 10 to 50 employees. No dedicated budget, no data scientist, no consultant. A daily irritant, a free or affordable tool, and a measurable result.

For each case: the problem, the solution, the real gain, and the pitfall to avoid.

## Case 1: Accounting: categorizing invoices

**The problem.** 2 hours a week sorting and categorizing supplier invoices in the accounting software. Repetitive, low-value work, prone to errors.

**The solution.** An AI assistant that reads the invoice (PDF or photo), extracts the amount, supplier, category, and pre-fills the software.

**The gain.** <span className="corail">8 hours per month</span>. The accountant focuses on analysis, not data entry.

**The pitfall.** Always check the categorizations. AI gets it wrong on ambiguous cases: multi-line invoices, credit notes, conditional discounts.


## Case 2: HR: responding to job applications

**The problem.** 50 applications per opening, copy-pasted replies that give a cold image of the company. Rejected candidates get a generic message or nothing at all.

**The solution.** An AI template that generates personalized responses in **30 seconds**, adapted to the candidate's profile and the position. The tone stays human because you wrote the base message.

**The gain.** Time freed up for interviews. And a <span className="corail">better employer brand</span>: even a rejection leaves a good impression when it's personalized.

**The pitfall.** Never send without proofreading. The tone must stay consistent with your company culture. AI writes, you sign.

## Case 3: Sales: preparing meetings

**The problem.** 30 minutes searching for prospect information before each meeting: website, LinkedIn, news, annual reports. And sometimes, you show up underprepared for lack of time.

**The solution.** An AI prompt that compiles in **2 minutes** a prospect brief: activity, recent news, likely challenges, questions to ask. You walk into the meeting with a structured brief.

**The gain.** Better-prepared meetings, a <span className="corail">higher conversion rate</span>, and a signal of professionalism sent to the prospect.

**The pitfall.** Verify the information. AI can hallucinate revenue figures, invent news, or confuse two companies with similar names.


## Case 4: Customer service: handling recurring requests

**The problem.** 60% of support requests are about the same 10 questions: delivery tracking, return conditions, warranty duration. Your team spends its time answering the same thing.

**The solution.** An AI chatbot trained on your FAQ that instantly answers simple questions and escalates complex cases to a human.

**The gain.** Response time <span className="corail">divided by 3</span>, higher customer satisfaction, and a support team focused on real problems.

**The pitfall.** The **transparency** obligation. The customer must know they're talking to an AI. It's in the **EU AI Act**, and it's also a matter of trust.

## Case 5: Management: summarizing meeting briefs

**The problem.** 15 emails, 3 documents, 2 reports to read before a management meeting. Nobody reads them all. The meeting starts with uneven levels of information.

**The solution.** An AI summary of everything in 1 minute: key points, expected decisions, alerts. Everyone arrives on the same page.

**The gain.** <span className="corail">20 minutes saved per meeting</span>. Discussions that focus on decisions, not context.

**The pitfall.** The summary is a starting point, not a substitute. For sensitive documents (contracts, audits, disputes), **reading the full document** remains essential.


## What's next?

These 5 cases have one thing in common: they cost <span className="corail">almost nothing</span> to test. A free tool (Claude, ChatGPT, Mistral), one hour of testing, and you know if it works for your company.

The real risk is not misusing AI. It's **not starting at all**.
