## Why This Time Is Different

The AI Act isn't just another European regulation. It's the world's first comprehensive legal framework for artificial intelligence, classifying AI systems by risk level: unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal.

Good news for SMBs: most of your use cases (chatbots, writing assistants, analytics tools) fall under "limited" or "minimal" risk. The obligations are proportionate.

The catch: even at limited risk, you have **transparency obligations**. If a customer interacts with an AI chatbot, they must know. If you use generative AI to produce content, same rule.

## What Europe Has Planned for SMBs

The AI Act doesn't forget small businesses. Three support mechanisms are built in:

**Regulatory sandboxes**: each country must create at least one by August 2026. SMEs get priority access, free of charge. It's a space to test your AI usage and verify compliance, guided by authorities.

**Simplified documentation**: the European Commission will publish SME-friendly technical documentation templates. No law firm required.

**Proportionate fees**: compliance costs must account for company size.
