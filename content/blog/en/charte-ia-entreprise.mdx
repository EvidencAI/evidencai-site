## Why a Policy, Not a Ban

The temptation is to ban AI outright. "No AI at the office." Simple, clear, and counterproductive.

Your best people are already using AI. They save time, they're more effective. Banning it means losing that advantage and pushing them to do it secretly.

A policy does the opposite: it says **yes, but with rules**. It protects the company while enabling innovation.

## 3 Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: The 20-page policy.** Nobody will read it. One page is enough. If you can't summarize your rules on one page, you haven't thought enough.

**Mistake 2: Listing tools by name.** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini... the list changes every month. Set principles ("no client data in unvalidated AI tools") rather than names.

**Mistake 3: Never updating it.** AI moves fast. A policy written in March is outdated by September if nobody reviews it. Plan quarterly revisions.

## Concrete Example: A 25-Person SMB

Stéphane guides SMBs through this process. For a 25-person wholesale company, the policy fit in 5 points on one A4 page. Writing time: 1 hour with Claude. Deployment: 15 minutes in a team meeting.
