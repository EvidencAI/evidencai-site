## The mistake 9 out of 10 SMBs make

When a business owner hears about AI, they immediately think <span className="corail">complex technology</span>, data scientist, big budget. And they postpone.

Yet the SMBs benefiting from AI today don't have dedicated tech teams. They simply identified a recurring problem and tested a free tool on it.

The secret is the method: <span className="corail">problem first, tool second</span>. The research converges on this point: [McKinsey's 2025 State of AI report](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai) shows that companies extracting real value from AI are not those stacking tools, but those starting from an identified business problem.

## The 3 classic traps at the start

**The toy trap**. You test ChatGPT on a joke, you're impressed, you buy the subscription for the whole team, nobody knows what to do with it, the subscription is canceled 3 months later. It cost you deployment time and a bad reputation for AI topics internally.

**The all-knowing vendor trap**. An agency offers you a custom AI chatbot for 25,000 euros. The quote looks serious. Six months later, the chatbot answers 3% of customer questions and nobody uses it. Money lost, trust too.

**The eternal POC trap**. You launch a pilot on a data subset. Everything works in the lab. But going to production requires touching the existing IS, training 30 people, revising processes. Energy fades, the project stalls.

In all three cases, the root cause is the same: <span className="corail">no clear business problem at the start</span>. Just a desire to adopt AI.

## The 4-step method

SMBs that succeed follow a simple protocol, which we call **PTMI** internally: Problem, Test, Measure, Iterate.

**Step 1: Problem**. A repetitive, time-consuming or annoying task an employee does every week. Not a strategic vision, a concrete pain point. A name, a task, a weekly duration.

**Step 2: Test**. An off-the-shelf tool, ideally free to start. Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini. A one-hour session with the employee concerned to test on a real case. Not a 3-month POC, one hour.

**Step 3: Measure**. Is it saving time? How much? Is the output quality acceptable? Is the employee comfortable with it? Three questions, three answers by end of week.

**Step 4: Iterate**. If yes, roll it out to the team with a <span className="corail">half-page user guide</span>. If no, change the tool or change the problem. No drama, no strategic meeting, just iterate.

The full cycle takes two weeks, not six months. You end up either with a validated use case the team adopts, or with a cheap lesson learned.

## 3 concrete examples (no science fiction)

**The accountant spending 2 hours a week categorizing invoices.** An AI assistant reads the PDF and suggests the category. The accountant validates or corrects. Gain: <span className="corail">8 hours per month</span>, zero software investment. Tool used: Claude or ChatGPT, free versions.

**The HR manager writing the same candidate rejection emails.** A template prompt generates personalized responses in 30 seconds from the CV and job description. Gain: time freed up for **interviews that matter**, more human tone than a copy-pasted email. Setup time: 1 hour to write the prompt, dialed in after a week.

**The CEO prepping for meetings by re-reading 15 emails.** An AI summary of the email thread in 1 click, with decision points extracted. Gain: <span className="corail">20 minutes per meeting</span>, every day. Tool: Copilot in Outlook if you're on Microsoft, otherwise Claude with copy-paste.

In all three cases, setup cost less than a day of work. No budget. No integrator. No legal risk, provided a few rules are respected on the data you hand to the tool.

## The 3 rules not to overlook

A successful start isn't just about productivity. Three guardrails to put in place from week one.

**Rule 1: no sensitive data in consumer tools**. No named customer data, no individual HR data, no detailed financial data in the free versions of ChatGPT or Claude. For those cases, enterprise versions exist with no-training clauses and European hosting. France's [CNIL](https://www.cnil.fr/en/artificial-intelligence) publishes up-to-date recommendations on GDPR precautions to take.

**Rule 2: human validation on decisions**. AI proposes, humans decide. A candidate rejection, a sensitive customer reply, a number going into a report: always reviewed by a human before sending or publication.

**Rule 3: track what works**. A shared document where the team logs the useful uses they discover. After 3 months, you own 10 to 15 validated use cases, without spending a euro on consulting.
